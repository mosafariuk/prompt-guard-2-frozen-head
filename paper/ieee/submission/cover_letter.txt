Dear Editor-in-Chief,

I am writing to submit my manuscript entitled "Multi-Layered Defensive Architecture
and Webhook Securitization for LLM Deployment in Multi-Tenant Enterprise Systems" for
consideration of publication in IEEE Access.

This research addresses a critical and rapidly expanding vulnerability in enterprise
SaaS platforms: the ingestion of machine-originated, attacker-influenceable webhooks
into shared Large Language Model (LLM) contexts. While existing literature primarily
focuses on prompt injection against conversational chatbots, this work targets the
multi-tenant webhook vector, where the risk of cross-tenant data leakage and execution
privilege elevation is severe.

The manuscript presents a vertically integrated, multi-layered defensive architecture
that relocates LLM webhook defense to the network edge. The core contributions are:

Cryptographic Tenant Isolation: I introduce and formally prove a webhook signing
scheme (binding tenant identifiers into HMAC-SHA256 signatures) that reduces
cross-tenant attribution failure to HMAC's multi-user EUF-CMA security, independent of
tenant count.

Sub-Millisecond Deterministic Screening: I demonstrate a V8-isolate edge firewall
using Aho-Corasick multi-pattern matching that executes well within the 10 ms edge CPU
budget, with orders-of-magnitude headroom.

Empirical Resolution of Model Calibration: The central empirical finding directly
challenges current assumptions about open-weight guardrails. I show that Meta's Prompt
Guard 2's native 22.8% out-of-distribution (OOD) recall is not a structural encoder
limitation, but a precision-first decision-head artifact. By training a linear logistic
head on the model's frozen penultimate embeddings under a strict, pre-registered,
leakage-controlled protocol, I achieve 99.9% OOD recall at a 0.7% false-positive rate
(AUC ~ 0.999). The full Phase-5 classifier pipeline has been open-sourced at
https://github.com/mosafariuk/prompt-guard-2-frozen-head.

This manuscript aligns with the multidisciplinary scope of IEEE Access, sitting at the
intersection of network security, applied cryptography, and artificial-intelligence
safety.
